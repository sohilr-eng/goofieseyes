On the way back from Tobago on the ferry, I saw this child sitting and looking longingly out the window. She was very hyper the entire ride, but as soon as we started moving she became silently infatuated with the town, a city in her eyes, and in the eyes of the residents of Tobago, I guess.
I thought it was just childhood curiosity, but as we came closer to Port of Spain, I overheard the child's mother speaking to her and her sibling, warning them about the grand scale of the city, "This is not like Scarborough, this is a big city, we need to be careful. The buildings are so large and there are so many people here."
The concept of considering Port of Spain such a large city was dumbfounding. There are 4 buildings that could potentially be considered skyscrapers in the capital of Trinidad, and none of them might even cross 10 stories.
What if this child was seeing New York City for the first time — how would she comprehend the scale? Where would her mother even begin to warn her?
Everyone sees the world from a very specific perspective, shaped by their experiences, nature, and biases cultivated over their years of living. This is such a beautiful concept, and this photo will always remind me of that.
